## 4. On-Page SEO Standards

### Every Page Must Have:
- **Title Tag:** Primary keyword + location + brand (under 60 chars)
  - Example: `Office Cleaning Chicago | Chicago Commercial Cleaner`
- **Meta Description:** Action-oriented, includes keyword + CTA (under 155 chars)
  - Example: `Professional office cleaning in Chicago. Licensed, insured & eco-friendly. Get your free quote today.`
- **H1:** One per page, includes primary keyword
- **H2s:** Include secondary/related keywords naturally
- **Image Alt Text:** Descriptive, keyword-rich where natural
- **Internal Links:** Minimum 3 per page (service <-> location cross-links)
- **Schema Markup:**
  - LocalBusiness schema on every page
  - FAQ schema on service pages
  - Review/AggregateRating schema on reviews page
  - BreadcrumbList schema sitewide
  - Service schema on service pages

### Content Rules:
- **ALL content must follow `docs/human-seo-writing-guide.md`** — the human-first writing rulebook. No exceptions.
- No duplicate content across location pages — each must have unique intro, local details, and area-specific info
- Every page needs a clear primary CTA (quote form or phone number)
- Use Chicago-specific language: "Chicagoland," neighborhood names, "the Loop," "L train," local landmarks
- Include trust signals: licensed, bonded, insured, eco-friendly badges
- Run every piece through the Banned Words/Phrases checklist before publishing
- Target 80%+ human score on AI detectors (Originality.ai or GPTZero)

## 10. GEO & AIO Strategy (AI Search Optimization)

> **Why this matters:** AI-referred web sessions jumped 527% YoY in 2025. Google AI Overviews now appear in 85%+ of searches. ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini are becoming how people find local services. If your content isn't structured for AI citation, you're invisible to a fast-growing channel.

### The Landscape: Where AI Pulls Answers From

| AI Engine | How It Finds You | What It Favors |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Google AI Overviews** | Pulls from top-ranking pages + structured data | Existing top-ranking content, schema markup, E-E-A-T signals |
| **ChatGPT (SearchGPT)** | Bing index + web browsing + training data | Encyclopedic authority content, E-E-A-T, clear structure |
| **Perplexity** | Real-time web crawl + citations | Recency, specific data/stats, machine-readable markup (schema, OpenGraph) |
| **Claude** | Web search when enabled | Well-structured, factual, citation-rich content |
| **Gemini** | Google index + Knowledge Graph | Google Business Profile data, schema, authoritative sources |

### Content Structure for AI Citation

Every page and blog post must follow the **"Short Answer + Deep Dive"** format:

**Rule 1: Answer First (First 40-60 Words)**
The opening of every page must directly answer the primary query. AI engines evaluate relevance primarily on opening content.

```
BAD:  "Welcome to Chicago Commercial Cleaner. We've been serving the 
       Chicagoland area with pride..."

GOOD: "Commercial cleaning in Chicago costs between $0.15-$0.45 per square 
       foot for standard office cleaning, with most businesses paying $500-$2,500/month. 
       Pricing depends on square footage, cleaning frequency, and service type."
```

**Rule 2: TL;DR Summaries Under Every H2**
Add a 1-2 sentence summary immediately after each section heading. This lets AI engines extract standalone answers from any section without needing full page context.

```markdown
## How Often Should a Chicago Office Be Cleaned?
**Quick answer:** Most Chicago offices need professional cleaning 2-3 times per 
week. High-traffic areas like lobbies and restrooms may need daily service.

[Then expand with full detail below...]
```

**Rule 3: Fact Density — Stats Every 150-200 Words**
AI models heavily favor content with specific, citable data. Vague claims get ignored.

```
BAD:  "Regular cleaning improves employee health."

GOOD: "Offices cleaned 3x/week see 46% fewer sick days according to ISSA 
       research. In Chicago, where flu season peaks November through March, 
       this translates to roughly 2.3 fewer absences per employee per year."
```

**Rule 4: Headers as Questions**
Reformatting H2/H3 headers as natural questions is one of the highest-ROI GEO changes. AI engines match user queries to question-formatted headers.

```
BAD:  ## Our Cleaning Process
GOOD: ## What Does a Commercial Cleaning Service Include?

BAD:  ## Pricing
GOOD: ## How Much Does Commercial Cleaning Cost in Chicago?

BAD:  ## Service Areas
GOOD: ## What Areas of Chicago Do You Serve?
```

**Rule 5: Structured Lists and Tables**
AI engines parse structured content more reliably than prose paragraphs. Use:
- Numbered lists for processes/steps
- Bullet lists for features/benefits
- Comparison tables for pricing/services
- Definition lists for terminology

### Local GEO: Winning "Near Me" + City Queries in AI

Key finding from research: queries WITHOUT a specific location name are 11% more likely to trigger AI Overviews than queries WITH location names. This means:

**Optimize for BOTH formats:**
```
"commercial cleaning near me" → AI Overview likely, pulls from GBP + local signals
"commercial cleaning Chicago" → Traditional SERP more likely, but still AIO-eligible
```

**For AI Overviews on local queries:**
- Google Business Profile completeness is critical (Gemini pulls directly from GBP)
- Review volume and recency matter — AI Overviews surface businesses with high review counts
- NAP consistency across citations reinforces entity recognition
- Location pages with embedded maps, local landmarks, and area-specific content get cited

**For ChatGPT/Perplexity on local queries:**
- These rely more on web content than GBP
- Create definitive "best commercial cleaning in [area]" content on your own site
- Earn mentions on third-party listicles (Expertise.com, Yelp, Angi)
- The more places your brand appears with consistent info, the more AI trusts you as a real entity

### Platform-Specific Playbook

**Google AI Overviews**

- Google Business Profile must be 100% complete with 25+ reviews

**ChatGPT / SearchGPT**
- Priority: Build entity authority across the web (not just your site)
- Get cited on Wikipedia (long-term play — start with local Chicago articles)
- Earn press mentions — ChatGPT heavily favors third-party coverage over brand content
- Detailed About page with author bios increases citation likelihood
- Don't block GPTBot in robots.txt

**Perplexity**
- Priority: Recency and structured data win here
- Perplexity crawls aggressively — keep content fresh with visible timestamps
- Schema.org, OpenGraph, and JSON-LD make content machine-readable
- Specific statistics and named sources get cited at dramatically higher rates
- Don't block PerplexityBot in robots.txt

**Gemini**
- Priority: Google ecosystem dominance
- Pulls heavily from Google Business Profile and Knowledge Graph
- Same content that ranks in Google Search ranks in Gemini
- Google Reviews are a primary signal
