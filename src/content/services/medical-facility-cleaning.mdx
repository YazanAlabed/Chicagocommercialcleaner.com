### What Does Medical Cleaning Include?

**TL;DR:** Exam room disinfection with proper contact time, waiting room sanitization, restroom deep cleaning, lab area cleaning, staff area maintenance, and documentation logs for compliance.

**Exam Room Cleaning**
We follow CDC guidelines. High-touch surfaces get hospital-grade disinfectant. Exam tables, chairs, light handles, sink faucets, door handles. We don't skip the things patients touch.

**Waiting Room Disinfection**
Waiting rooms host sick patients. Chairs, magazines, check-in counters, door handles. We disinfect thoroughly and frequently.

**Restroom Sanitation**
Medical restrooms require more than standard cleaning. Hospital-grade disinfectants. Focus on high-touch surfaces. Check for plumbing issues. ADA compliance checks.

**Lab and Specimen Areas**
Labs require careful cleaning. Blood draw stations, centrifuge areas, sample storage. We follow proper protocols.

**Staff Areas**
Break rooms, staff lounges, locker rooms. We clean with the same attention as patient areas. Cross-contamination happens in staff areas too.

### What Cleaning Protocols Do We Follow?

**TL;DR:** We use EPA-registered hospital-grade disinfectants, follow proper contact times (not spray-and-wipe), train all staff on HIPAA and bloodborne pathogens, and provide cleaning logs for compliance.

**EPA-Registered Disinfectants**
We use products approved for healthcare settings. These kill common pathogens including MRSA, C. difficile, influenza, and bloodborne pathogens.

**Proper Contact Time**
Disinfectants need time to work. We follow manufacturer contact times. Spray, wait, wipe. Not spray and immediately wipe.

**Bloodborne Pathogen Protocols**
Blood and bodily fluids require specific handling. OSHA standards. Proper PPE. Correct disposal methods.

**Cross-Contamination Prevention**
Different cleaning materials for different areas. We don't clean an exam room and then use the same cloth in the waiting room. Color-coded systems.
