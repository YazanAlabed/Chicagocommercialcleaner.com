### Why Do Restaurants Need Specialized Cleaning?

**TL;DR:** Health inspectors show up unannounced and check for rodent signs, grease buildup, food temperatures, and employee hygiene. A failed inspection means a posted grade that customers see. Regular deep cleaning prevents violations.

Chicago health inspectors don't call ahead. They check for rodent signs, grease behind equipment, food temperatures, and employee hygiene. One failed inspection can cost thousands in fines and lost customers.

### What Do Chicago Health Inspectors Check?

**TL;DR:** Inspectors check food temperatures, employee hygiene, pest evidence, equipment cleanliness, plumbing, and chemical storage. The most common violations are improper temperatures, poor hygiene, and pest evidence.

**Food Temperature (Critical)**
Hot holding at 135°F or above. Cold holding at 41°F or below. Cooking temperatures verified. Cooling procedures documented.

**Employee Hygiene (Critical)**
Hand washing compliance. No bare hand contact with ready-to-eat food. Proper glove use. Employee illness policy.

**Pest Evidence (Critical)**
No rodent droppings. No cockroach sightings. No flies in food areas. Proper pest control documentation.

**Equipment Cleanliness**
Food contact surfaces clean. Grease buildup removed. Cutting boards in good condition. No food residue.
