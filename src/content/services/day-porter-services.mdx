### What Is a Day Porter?

**TL;DR:** A day porter is an on-site cleaner who works during business hours, providing continuous cleaning and immediate response to issues. Unlike night cleaning crews, day porters are present when employees and visitors are there.

Day porters handle what happens during business hours. Spills in the lobby. Restroom restocking. Trash overflow. Immediate cleaning needs. They're your on-site cleaning presence while your business operates.

**What Makes Day Porters Different:**
- Present during business hours
- Immediate response to spills and issues
- Continuous maintenance throughout the day
- Visible, professional presence
- Flexible task handling

**TL;DR:** Restroom restocking and cleaning, lobby maintenance, spill cleanup, trash removal, entrance maintenance, event setup/breakdown, and immediate response to cleaning needs.

### What's the Difference Between Day Porter and Night Cleaning?

**TL;DR:** Day porters work during business hours, handling ongoing maintenance and immediate issues. Night cleaning crews do deep cleaning when the building is empty. Most businesses need both.

**Day Porter:**
- Works during business hours
- Handles immediate issues
- Maintains restrooms continuously
- Visible presence
- Light cleaning tasks

**Night Cleaning:**
- Works after hours
- Deep cleaning tasks
- Vacuuming, mopping, trash
- Empty building
- Thorough cleaning

**Most businesses use both:**
Day porter maintains throughout the day. Night cleaning does deep work. Together they keep facilities clean and presentable.
