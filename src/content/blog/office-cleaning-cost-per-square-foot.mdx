## Office Cleaning Costs: Chicago 2026

Cleaning costs per square foot vary based on size, frequency, building type, and location. This guide breaks down Chicago pricing for 2026 so you can budget accurately.

**Quick Answer:** Office cleaning in Chicago costs $0.10-$0.35 per square foot per month for standard service. A 5,000 sq ft office typically pays $500-$1,750/month. Deep cleaning adds $0.20-$0.50 per square foot. Medical offices and high-security facilities cost more.

### What Is the Base Pricing by Office Size?

**TL;DR:** Small offices (under 2,500 sq ft) pay $0.25-$0.40/sq ft/month. Medium offices (2,500-10,000 sq ft) pay $0.15-$0.30/sq ft. Large offices (10,000-50,000 sq ft) pay $0.10-$0.25/sq ft. Economies of scale reduce per-square-foot costs.

**Small Offices (Under 2,500 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.25-$0.40/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $300-$1,000
- Why higher per sq ft? Minimum charges apply. Fixed costs spread over small area.

**Medium Offices (2,500-10,000 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.15-$0.30/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $375-$3,000
- Most common size. Competitive pricing available.

**Large Offices (10,000-50,000 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.10-$0.25/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $1,000-$12,500
- Economies of scale. Better per-square-foot rates.

**Very Large (50,000+ sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.08-$0.20/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: Custom quote
- Requires custom pricing. Volume discounts.

### What's Included in Standard Cleaning Pricing?

**TL;DR:** Standard pricing includes vacuuming, mopping, trash removal, restroom cleaning and restocking, break room surface wiping, spot cleaning, weekly dusting, and entry glass cleaning. NOT included: carpet extraction, floor refinishing, window washing beyond entries, upholstery cleaning.

**Daily or Regular Service:**
- Vacuum carpets
- Mop hard floors
- Empty trash and recycling
- Clean and restock restrooms
- Wipe break room surfaces
- Spot clean spills
- Dust horizontal surfaces (weekly)
- Clean glass entry doors

**What's NOT Included:**
- Carpet extraction (deep cleaning)
- Floor stripping and refinishing
- Window washing (beyond entry doors)
- Upholstery cleaning
- Deep cleaning beyond standard scope

### What Factors Affect Cleaning Pricing?

**TL;DR:** Frequency (daily vs. weekly), building type (medical offices cost 20-40% more), location (downtown Chicago adds 10-25%), condition (poor condition increases initial costs), and special services (day porters add $2,000-$4,000/month) all affect pricing.

**Frequency:**
- Daily cleaning: Base price
- 3x/week: 70-80% of daily price
- 2x/week: 50-60% of daily price
- Weekly: 30-40% of daily price

**Building Type:**
- Standard office: Base price
- Medical office: 20-40% premium
- Industrial/warehouse: 10-30% less per sq ft (larger spaces)
- High-security: 10-20% premium
- Historic building: 10-20% premium (careful cleaning needed)

**Location:**
- Downtown/Loop: 10-20% premium (parking, access, security)
- Suburban office parks: Base price
- Industrial areas: 5-10% less

**Condition:**
- Well-maintained: Base price
- Poor condition: One-time deep clean + higher ongoing rate
- New construction: Standard pricing (post-construction clean extra)

**Services Included:**
- Standard list: Base price
- Day porter: $2,000-$4,000/month additional
- After-hours only: 10-20% premium
- Weekend cleaning: 15-25% premium

### How Does Location Affect Pricing in Chicago?

**TL;DR:** Downtown/Loop offices pay 15-25% more due to parking, security access, and after-hours requirements. West Loop/River North adds 10-20%. Suburban office parks have no premium. Industrial areas may be lower due to economies of scale.

**Downtown/Loop:**
- Premium: 15-25% higher
- Why: Parking costs, security access, building restrictions, after-hours requirements

**West Loop/River North:**
- Premium: 10-20% higher
- Why: Similar to Loop but slightly less restricted

**Suburban Office Parks:**
- Premium: None (base pricing)
- Why: Easy access, parking, standard buildings

**Industrial Areas:**
- Premium: None (may be lower)
- Why: Larger spaces, simpler cleaning, economies of scale

### How Do You Get Accurate Cleaning Quotes?

**TL;DR:** Request on-site visits (phone quotes are estimates), provide detailed information (exact square footage, restroom count, flooring types), ask for itemized quotes, and ensure all quotes include the same scope for fair comparison.

**Request On-Site Visits:**
Phone quotes are estimates. On-site visits give accurate pricing. Cleaners need to see the space.

**Provide Detailed Information:**
- Square footage (exact)
- Number of restrooms
- Number of break rooms
- Flooring types
- Frequency desired
- Special requirements

**Ask for Itemized Quotes:**
Monthly service, special services, and additional charges should be separate line items.

**Compare Apples to Apples:**
Make sure all quotes include the same scope. One quote may be lower because it includes less.

### What Questions Affect Your Cleaning Price?

**TL;DR:** Square footage, restroom count, flooring types, cleaning frequency, building hours (after-hours costs more), and whether you need day porter service all affect pricing. Accurate answers get accurate quotes.

**What's your square footage?**
Larger spaces get better per-square-foot rates. Measure accurately.

**How many restrooms?**
More restrooms = more cleaning time. Number of fixtures affects pricing.

**What flooring do you have?**
Carpet and hard floors require different cleaning. Carpet needs extraction; hard floors need refinishing.

**How often do you need cleaning?**
Daily is more expensive than 2-3x/week, but sometimes necessary.

**What are your hours?**
After-hours cleaning may cost more. Early morning is usually standard.

**Do you need day porter service?**
Day porters cost $2,000-$4,000/month additional. Not always needed.

### Can You Negotiate Cleaning Prices?

**TL;DR:** You can negotiate frequency, scope (remove unnecessary tasks), contract length, and multi-location volume discounts. You can't negotiate base costs (labor, insurance) without sacrificing quality. Get 3-5 quotes to compare.

**You Can Negotiate:**
- Frequency (reduce to lower cost)
- Scope (remove tasks you don't need)
- Contract length (longer terms may reduce price)
- Multiple locations (volume discount)

**You Can't Negotiate:**
- Base costs (labor, supplies, insurance)
- Quality (paying less shouldn't mean lower quality)

**Get Multiple Quotes:**
3-5 quotes give you comparison. Be wary of quotes significantly lower than others.
