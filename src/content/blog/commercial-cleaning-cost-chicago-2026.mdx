## How Much Does Commercial Cleaning Cost in Chicago?

**Quick Answer:** Chicago commercial cleaning costs $0.10-$0.35 per square foot per month. A 5,000 sq ft office typically pays $500-$1,750/month. Medical offices cost 20-40% more. Downtown locations cost 15-25% more than suburbs.

### What's Included in Standard Cleaning Pricing?

**TL;DR:** Standard cleaning includes vacuuming, mopping, trash removal, restroom cleaning, surface wiping, and break room maintenance. Deep cleaning, window washing, and carpet extraction cost extra.

**Daily or Regular Service:**
- Vacuum carpets
- Mop hard floors
- Empty trash and recycling
- Clean and restock restrooms
- Wipe break room surfaces
- Spot clean spills
- Dust horizontal surfaces (weekly)
- Clean glass entry doors

**What's NOT Included:**
- Carpet extraction (deep cleaning)
- Floor stripping and refinishing
- Window washing (beyond entry doors)
- Upholstery cleaning
- Deep cleaning beyond standard scope

### How Does Pricing Vary by Office Size?

**TL;DR:** Smaller offices pay more per square foot due to minimum charges. Larger offices get volume discounts. A 2,000 sq ft office pays $300-$800/month; a 10,000 sq ft office pays $900-$2,500/month.

**Small Offices (Under 2,500 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.25-$0.40/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $300-$1,000
- Why higher per sq ft? Minimum charges apply. Fixed costs spread over small area.

**Medium Offices (2,500-10,000 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.15-$0.30/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $375-$3,000
- Most common size. Competitive pricing available.

**Large Offices (10,000-50,000 sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.10-$0.25/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: $1,000-$12,500
- Economies of scale. Better per-square-foot rates.

**Very Large (50,000+ sq ft):**
- Rate: $0.08-$0.20/sq ft/month
- Monthly cost: Custom quote
- Requires custom pricing. Volume discounts.

### What Additional Services Cost Extra?

**TL;DR:** Carpet extraction costs $0.20-$0.40/sq ft quarterly. Floor stripping runs $0.50-$1.00/sq ft annually. Window washing is $2-$5 per window. Budget $1,000-$3,000/year for special services beyond standard cleaning.

| Carpet extraction | $0.20-$0.40/sq ft | Quarterly or as needed |
| Floor stripping/refinishing | $0.50-$1.00/sq ft | Annually |
| Window washing (interior) | $2-$5/window | Monthly or quarterly |

| Deep cleaning (entire space) | $0.20-$0.50/sq ft | Quarterly |

### How Does Building Type Affect Pricing?

**TL;DR:** Medical offices cost 20-40% more due to HIPAA compliance and medical-grade disinfectants. Industrial facilities cost 10-30% less per sq ft due to larger spaces. High-security buildings cost 10-20% more.

**Standard office:** Base price
**Medical office:** +20-40% premium for HIPAA compliance and medical-grade disinfectants
**Industrial/warehouse:** -10-30% per sq ft (larger spaces, lower per-sq-ft rate)
**High-security:** +10-20% premium for security protocols and clearance
**Historic building:** +10-20% premium for careful cleaning of original finishes

### Why Does Downtown Chicago Cost More?

**TL;DR:** Downtown cleaning costs 15-25% more than suburbs due to parking ($15-$40/visit), building security requirements, freight elevator access, and after-hours scheduling.

**Downtown/Loop Premium: 15-25% higher**
Why: Parking costs, security access, building restrictions, after-hours requirements

**West Loop/River North Premium: 10-20% higher**
Why: Similar to Loop but slightly less restricted

**Suburban Office Parks:** Base pricing
Why: Easy access, free parking, standard buildings

**Example:**
- 5,000 sq ft suburban office: $800-$1,200/month
- 5,000 sq ft downtown office: $920-$1,500/month

### How Do I Calculate My Cleaning Budget?

**TL;DR:** Multiply square footage by rate ($0.15-$0.25/sq ft), add location premium if downtown, add 10-20% for annual services. A 5,000 sq ft suburban office budgets $1,000-$1,200/month.

**Step 1: Calculate Base Monthly Cost**
Square footage × Rate = Monthly cost

Example: 5,000 sq ft × $0.20/sq ft = $1,000/month

**Step 2: Add Location Premium (if applicable)**
- Downtown: +15-25%
- Medical: +20-40%

Example: $1,000 × 1.20 (downtown) = $1,200/month

**Step 3: Add Annual Services**
- Carpet extraction (quarterly): $500-$800/year
- Window washing (quarterly): $200-$500/year
- Floor refinishing (annual): $500-$1,000/year

Example: $600/year ÷ 12 = $50/month

**Step 4: Total Monthly Budget**
Base + Location Premium + Annual Services

Example: $1,200 + $50 = $1,250/month

### How Do I Get Accurate Quotes?

**TL;DR:** Request on-site visits for accurate pricing. Provide square footage, restroom count, flooring types, desired frequency, and special requirements. Get 3-5 quotes for comparison.

**Request On-Site Visits:**
Phone quotes are estimates. On-site visits give accurate pricing. Cleaners need to see the space.

**Provide Same Information:**
Give each company the same square footage, requirements, and frequency. This ensures apples-to-apples comparison.

**Ask for Itemized Quotes:**
Monthly service, special services, and additional charges should be separate line items.

**Compare Scope:**
Make sure each quote includes the same tasks. One quote may be lower because it includes less.

### What Questions Should I Ask for Accurate Quotes?

**TL;DR:** Provide exact square footage, restroom count, flooring types, desired frequency, and building access details. These factors most affect pricing accuracy.

When requesting quotes, provide:

- Exact square footage
- Number of restrooms and fixtures
- Flooring types by area
- Desired frequency
- Building access (parking, security)
- Current condition
- Any special requirements

### How Do I Compare Quotes Fairly?

**TL;DR:** Quotes should be within 20-30% of each other. Wildly low quotes cut corners. High quotes may include unnecessary services. Compare scope, not just price.

**Apples to Apples:**
Make sure all quotes include the same scope. One may be lower because it includes less.

**Watch For:**
- Supplies included or extra?
- Frequency the same?
- Additional charges?
- Contract length differences?

**Price Range:**
Quotes within 20-30% of each other are normal. Wildly low quotes are suspicious. High quotes may include unnecessary services.
