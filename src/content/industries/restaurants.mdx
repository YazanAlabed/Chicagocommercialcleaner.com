### Chicago Health Code Requirements

**What Inspectors Check**

The Chicago Department of Public Health sends inspectors to check:

- **Food temperature control** — Refrigeration, hot holding, cooling procedures
- **Food protection** — Storage, cross-contamination prevention, labeling
- **Equipment cleanliness** — Grease buildup, food residue, cutting boards
- **Plumbing and water** — Hot water availability, proper drainage, backflow prevention
- **Pest control** — Rodent signs, insect presence, exclusion methods
- **Chemical storage** — Proper labeling, separation from food, MSDS availability
- **Employee hygiene** — Hand-washing stations, glove use, illness policies

**Common Violations**

The most cited violations in Chicago restaurants:

1. **Improper food temperature** — Hot foods not hot enough, cold foods too warm
2. **Poor employee hygiene** — Missing hand-washing, bare hand contact with food
3. **Pest evidence** — Rodent droppings, cockroach sightings, flies
4. **Equipment cleaning failures** — Grease buildup, food residue, dirty surfaces
5. **Chemical violations** — Improper storage, missing labels, no MSDS

### Frequently Asked Questions

**How often should a restaurant kitchen be deep cleaned?**
Most Chicago restaurants need monthly deep cleaning for high-volume operations. Lower-volume establishments can extend to every 6-8 weeks. We recommend quarterly minimum for health code compliance.

We clean the area around grease traps and floor drains. We don't pump grease traps — that requires a licensed plumber. We recommend professional grease trap pumping every 1-3 months depending on volume.
