### Why Medical Cleaning Is Different

A medical office isn't just another office. Exam rooms hold traces of blood, bodily fluids, and pathogens that regular cleaning misses. Waiting rooms host patients with compromised immune systems who can't afford exposure. Staff areas need protection from cross-contamination.

We've cleaned medical offices across Chicago since 2019. We understand the difference between "looks clean" and "is safe." The first takes a paper towel. The second takes training, proper chemicals, and specific protocols.

**Exam Room Cleaning**
We follow CDC guidelines for healthcare facilities. High-touch surfaces get hospital-grade disinfectant. Exam tables. Chairs. Light handles. Sink faucets. Door handles. We don't skip the things patients touch.

### Frequently Asked Questions

**What disinfectants do you use in medical facilities?**
We use EPA-registered hospital-grade disinfectants appropriate for healthcare settings. These kill common healthcare pathogens including MRSA, C. diff, and influenza. We follow proper contact times.

**Are your cleaners trained for medical environments?**
Yes. Staff assigned to medical facilities receive training on HIPAA requirements, bloodborne pathogen standards, and proper disinfection protocols. We don't send general cleaners into medical offices.

**How do you handle sharps containers?**
We don't dispose of sharps containers — that's your clinical staff's responsibility. We clean around them without touching them. If a sharps container appears full, we notify your staff.

**Can you clean during patient hours?**
We can, but most medical offices prefer after-hours cleaning. Waiting rooms and common areas can be cleaned during the day. Exam rooms should be cleaned after hours or between patients when possible.

**Do you provide documentation for compliance?**
Yes. We provide cleaning checklists, chemical logs, and inspection reports. These documents support your compliance requirements.
